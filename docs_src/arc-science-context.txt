## Saving Money and Budgets

Money is saved when a person spends less than they earn. Reducing expenses is a common and effective way to save money. Eating lunch at home or bringing food from home instead of eating out at restaurants saves money because restaurant meals cost more than home-cooked meals. Using fewer resources, spending less, and cutting discretionary costs all cause money to be saved. Budgeting means tracking income and expenses to find places to reduce spending and increase savings. Quitting lunch out reduces the amount of money spent on food, which directly increases savings.

## Tree Roots and Sidewalks

Trees grow by extending their root systems underground to seek water and nutrients. As a tree matures and grows taller, its roots spread outward and downward beneath the soil surface. When a tree is planted near a sidewalk or pavement, the roots of that tree will eventually grow beneath and push against the concrete. The roots exert strong upward and lateral pressure as they grow, which can crack and heave concrete sidewalks and pavement. Concrete is a rigid material that cannot flex, so even slow-growing roots will eventually break or buckle the concrete above them. This is a common problem in urban environments where trees are planted close to paved areas. The roots of a tree located near a sidewalk might crack and break the concrete over time.

## Survival, Population Size, and Communities

Living in groups improves survival chances for both humans and animals. Larger groups can share resources, divide labor, warn each other of dangers, and cooperate to meet challenges. In a town, humans live close together, which allows them to share food, shelter, medical care, and social support. Towns provide infrastructure such as hospitals, fire stations, and food supply chains that individual people or isolated households cannot maintain alone. As the number of organisms in a group increases, the chance of survival of each individual organism generally increases because of shared resources and mutual protection. Humans living in a town have access to many more resources and protections than people living in isolation.

## Hawks, Predators, and Their Prey

Hawks are birds of prey, also called raptors. They are predatory birds that hunt and eat other animals, including small mammals such as mice and rabbits, as well as reptiles like lizards and snakes, and sometimes insects and frogs. Red-tailed hawks are large North American raptors commonly found in open fields, deserts, and woodlands. Hawks use their sharp talons to catch prey and their curved beaks to tear meat. Geckos are small lizards and are a common prey item for hawks. Hawks eat lizards, including geckos. When a red-tailed hawk is hunting, it will swoop down on small animals such as rodents and lizards that it spots from the air. Raptors have excellent eyesight that allows them to spot prey from great distances and altitudes.

## Wildfires, Drought, and Dry Vegetation

Wildfires are large, uncontrolled fires that spread through forests, grasslands, and shrublands. Drought is a prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall that causes soil and vegetation to dry out. Drought may cause wildfires because it dries out vegetation, turning leaves, grasses, and twigs into highly flammable fuel. Dry, parched foliage is much more likely to ignite and sustain fire than moist, hydrated plants. When plants are dry from lack of rainfall, a single spark or lightning strike can ignite a wildfire that spreads rapidly. The combination of drought conditions, strong winds, and parched vegetation greatly increases the chance and severity of wildfires. Drought-stressed vegetation is often described as tinder-dry, meaning it ignites easily.

## Biofuels and Electricity Production

Biofuels are fuels made from organic materials such as plant matter, agricultural waste, wood, or animal fats. When biofuels are burned, they release energy in the form of heat. Biofuel is used to produce electricity by burning it in generators or turbines that convert heat energy into electrical energy. This electricity can then be used to power homes, including running lights, appliances, and heating systems. Powering the lights in a home is a positive and practical use of electricity generated from biofuels. Unlike fossil fuels, biofuels come from renewable biological sources and can be grown or produced again. Burning biofuels to generate electricity is a positive application of this energy source.

## Solar Panels and Sunlight Conversion

Solar panels, also called photovoltaic panels, are devices that convert sunlight into electricity using the photovoltaic effect. When sunlight hits the solar cells in a panel, it excites electrons and generates an electric current. A solar panel converts sunlight into electricity that can power homes and appliances. Solar energy is a renewable and natural power source that does not require fuel from a traditional electric utility company. Installing solar panels on a home allows residents to generate their own electricity from natural sunlight, reducing or eliminating dependence on a conventional electric company. Solar panels collect sunlight and produce electrical power, making them an effective solution for generating natural power at home.

## Conservation and Fossil Fuels

Conservation means using natural resources carefully, reducing waste, and protecting the environment. One major example of conservation is reducing or avoiding the use of fossil fuels such as gasoline, coal, and natural gas. Fossil fuels are non-renewable energy sources extracted from the earth that release carbon dioxide when burned. An example of conservation is not using fossil fuel, such as choosing not to drive a gasoline-powered vehicle, or using public transportation. Avoiding the use of gasoline directly conserves fossil fuel resources and reduces air pollution. Conservation efforts include using less energy, recycling materials, and replacing fossil fuels with renewable alternatives such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power.

## Penguin Feathers and Insulation

Penguins, which belong to the family Spheniscidae, are flightless seabirds that live in cold climates, especially in Antarctica and surrounding regions. Penguins have a unique feather structure: their feathers are short, dense, and tightly packed to form a waterproof and insulating layer. Thick feathers can be used for keeping warm because they trap a layer of air close to the body, which insulates the animal against cold temperatures. The dense feather coat of penguins is one of their most important adaptations for surviving in cold environments. For Spheniscidae (penguins), their thick feathers serve the critical function of keeping them warm in frigid air and icy water. The feathers are so tightly layered that they also repel water, keeping the penguin's skin dry.

## Light Refraction, Prisms, and Diamonds

Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one medium into another with a different density. A prism is a transparent optical object, often made of glass, that refracts light and separates white light into its component colors, producing a spectrum or rainbow. A prism refracts light, which means it bends the light rays at different angles depending on their wavelength. This causes white light to split into the visible spectrum of red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet. Diamonds have a crystalline structure that also refracts and reflects light in a similar way to a prism. Shining light through a diamond can produce a brilliant display of color, also called dispersion, because the diamond refracts and scatters different wavelengths of light in different directions. This is why diamonds sparkle and produce colorful flashes of light when illuminated.

## Friction, Brakes, and Stopping Vehicles

Friction is a force that resists motion between two surfaces that are in contact. When objects rub against each other, friction converts kinetic energy into heat and slows the motion. Friction is used for stopping a vehicle by brakes. Car brakes work by pressing brake pads against the rotating wheels or brake discs, creating friction that slows and stops the wheels from turning. Dry brake pads have more friction with the wheel surface than wet brake pads, which means they can stop a vehicle more quickly. A wheel with dry brake pads will stop a car faster than one with wet brake pads because the dry surface generates more friction. The more friction created between the brake pad and the wheel, the more quickly a moving vehicle can be brought to a stop.

## Plants and Sunlight for Growth

Plants are living organisms that produce their own food through photosynthesis. Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to manufacture glucose for energy and growth. A plant requires sunlight to grow because sunlight is the energy source that drives photosynthesis. Without adequate sunlight, plants cannot produce enough food to sustain growth and will weaken or die. Shafts of sunlight provide the light energy that plants need to convert water and carbon dioxide into sugars. Both the quantity and quality of sunlight affect how well plants grow. Plants grown in bright, direct sunlight typically grow faster and more vigorously than plants grown in shade. Sunlight is one of the three fundamental requirements for plant growth, along with water and nutrients from soil.

## Eye Color and Genetic Inheritance

Eye color is a physical trait that is determined by genetics, specifically by the amount and type of melanin pigment in the iris of the eye. Eye color is an inherited characteristic, which means it is passed down from parents to children through genes. The genes that control eye color are inherited from both the mother and the father. Different combinations of these inherited genes produce different eye colors, ranging from brown, hazel, green, and blue. To predict a baby's eye color, the best method is to examine the genealogy records or family history of the parents, grandparents, and other relatives. The genealogy records of a baby's family provide information about which eye colors are present in the family's genetic history, which helps predict the baby's likely eye color. Because eye color is an inherited trait, family history is the most reliable guide for predicting it.

## The Moon, Craters, and Impact History

The Moon is the natural satellite of Earth. The surface of the Moon is covered with circular depressions called craters. The lunar surface contains many craters formed by the impact of asteroids, meteoroids, and comets over billions of years. When a space rock collides with the Moon's surface at high speed, the impact excavates a circular depression and ejects debris in all directions, forming a crater. The Moon has no atmosphere to burn up incoming space debris and no geological activity to erase impact craters, so these formations have accumulated and been preserved for billions of years. The characteristic pitted and cratered appearance of the lunar surface is explained by the many collisions that have occurred throughout the Moon's history. Impact craters are the most prominent and distinctive geological features of the Moon.

## Heat Transfer and Cold Objects

Heat is a form of energy that flows from warmer objects to cooler objects. When a warm or hot substance comes into contact with a cold object, heat energy transfers from the warm substance to the cold object, causing the warm substance to cool down. If a hot substance touches a cold object then that substance will likely cool because heat flows from higher-temperature objects to lower-temperature objects until equilibrium is reached. A refrigerator is a cold appliance that maintains a low temperature inside its compartment. If a person puts their arm inside a refrigerator, the arm is warmer than the refrigerator interior, so heat flows from the arm to the cold air inside, making the arm feel cold. Cold objects absorb heat from warmer objects that contact them, causing the warmer object to lose heat and cool down.

## Pollution and Pollutants in the Environment

Pollution refers to the introduction of harmful substances or contaminants into the natural environment, including air, water, and soil. Pollution is a source of pollutants, which are the specific substances that contaminate and damage ecosystems. Pollutants can be chemical substances, waste materials, or foreign objects introduced into an environment where they cause harm. Plastic bags floating in the ocean are an example of pollution and environmental pollutants because plastic is a non-biodegradable waste material that harms marine ecosystems. Plastic waste in the ocean can injure marine animals, disrupt food chains, and persist in the environment for hundreds of years. Other examples of pollution include industrial chemicals dumped in rivers, exhaust emissions from vehicles in the air, and agricultural chemicals in groundwater. Plastic bags in the ocean represent a clear and visible example of how human-produced pollutants harm the natural environment.

## Human Reproduction and Live Birth

Human reproduction requires both a male and a female to contribute genetic material that combines to form an embryo. Human females carry embryos and fetuses internally in an organ called the uterus, commonly referred to as the womb. A womb is the reproductive organ where the developing offspring grows during pregnancy. Live birth means developing inside the mother instead of inside an egg, which is characteristic of mammals including humans. Human reproduction requires a womb because the developing fetus must grow inside the mother's uterus, where it receives nourishment through the placenta, for approximately nine months before being born. This is different from species that lay eggs, where the offspring develops outside the mother's body. Humans are viviparous, meaning they give birth to live offspring that developed inside the mother.

## Global Temperature, Glaciers, and Climate Change

Glaciers are large masses of compacted ice and snow that form in cold regions and at high altitudes over long periods of time. Glaciers are sensitive to temperature changes in the environment. If the atmospheric temperature rises then the glaciers will melt, because ice converts from a solid to a liquid when it is warmed above freezing point. Rising global temperatures cause glaciers to lose mass and retreat as ice melts faster than new snow accumulates. This process is similar to what happens in a living room when someone turns up the room heater: the heat in the air increases and causes solid objects to warm and melt. Melting glaciers contribute to rising sea levels and changes in freshwater availability worldwide. The atmospheric temperature is the primary environmental factor that determines whether glaciers grow, shrink, or remain stable.

## Gas Expansion and Heat in Flexible Containers

Gases respond to temperature changes by expanding when heated and contracting when cooled. This relationship between temperature and gas volume is described by Charles's Law, which states that the volume of a gas increases proportionally with temperature when pressure is held constant. As heat increases, a flexible container containing gas will expand because the gas molecules move faster at higher temperatures and push outward against the container walls with more force. Balloons are flexible containers filled with gas. When balloons heat up, the gas inside them expands, causing the balloon to get bigger and inflate further. Conversely, when balloons cool down, the gas contracts and the balloon shrinks. This is why balloons left in a warm car or brought out into the sun appear larger than balloons in cool environments. The heat energy causes the gas molecules to move with greater energy, expanding the balloon's volume.

## Eggs, Hatching, and Animal Birth

Many animals reproduce by laying eggs. An egg is a protective structure that contains a developing embryo and nutrients needed for growth until the young animal is ready to emerge. When the eggs hatch, the offspring are born — that is, when an egg hatches, the animal inside has completed its embryonic development and emerges as a new living individual. If an animal hatches from an egg then that animal is born at the moment of hatching. Hatching is the birth process for egg-laying species such as birds, reptiles, fish, amphibians, and insects. The offspring that emerges from an egg is a newly born animal, even though it developed inside a shell rather than inside its mother's body. After hatching, the newborn animal begins its independent life outside the egg.
